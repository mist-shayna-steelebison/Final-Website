Geography of Punjab

Explore land, rivers, climate, and agriculture. Expand each section to reveal key facts.

Rivers — The Five Tributaries

The region’s name is often linked to the “land of five waters”: Jhelum, Chenab, Ravi, Beas, and Sutlej. Canals and irrigation shape settlement and agriculture.

Climate — Seasons & Monsoon

Hot summers, a monsoon season, and mild winters influence crop choices and cultural rhythms (e.g., harvest festivals).

Terrain — Plains & Foothills

Fertile alluvial plains dominate; the Shivalik foothills rise in the northeast. Urban centers expand around transport corridors.

Agriculture — Breadbasket of the Subcontinent

Major crops include wheat, rice, sugarcane, and cotton; dairy and horticulture add diversity.
